Chaos

A dynamic system is an ordinary differential equation with several spacial dimensions. These examples are all first order equations in two spacial dimensions, which means the position of a point completely determines its velocity. 

      x'(t) = fx(x(t), y(t), t)
      y'(t) = fy(x(t), y(t), t)

You can think of the the field being a flow of water. We are going to drop some motes of dust into the water and watch where they flow. This is not a great analogy because water is not very compressible, and these flows are.

The first few examples are quite simple. The last one shows some interesting behavior. Scroll to the bottom for some recommended reading.
